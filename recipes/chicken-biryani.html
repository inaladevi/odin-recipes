Hyderabadi Chicken Biryani

Welcome! If you're here, you're probably in the mood for a warm, comforting plate of Chicken Biryani. It is one of those dishes that feels special every time you make it. The tender chicken, fragrant rice, and gentle blend of spices come together to create a meal that's as comforting as it is flavorful.

This recipe is designed to be easy to follow, with clear steps and simple ingredients you can find in most kitchens. Whether you're cooking for family, friends, or just yourself, this biryani will make the occasion feel a little brighter.

Did You Know?

Hyderabadi biryani first appeared in the royal kitchens of the Nizams in the 18th century.   • 1 bay leaf, 2 green cardamoms, 2 cloves, 1 small cinnamon stick, 1 star anise
  • 1 tsp oil or ghee

For Layering

  • ¬º cup chopped mint leaves
  • ¬º cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 2 Tbsp fried onions (birista)
  • a few saffron strands soaked in 2-3 Tbsp warm milk
  • 2 Tbsp warm ghee

Preparation

Step 1: Marinate the Chicken

In a large bowl, combine chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, lemon juice, salt, slit green chilies, mint, coriander, and 2 Tbsp pre-fried onions. Mix well, cover, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour (overnight for best flavor).

Step 2: Prepare the Fried Onions (Birista)

Heat ghee or oil in a deep pan. Add thinly sliced onions and fry on medium heat, stirring often, until deep golden brown and crisp. Remove and drain on paper towels. These will be used in both marination and layering.

Step 3: Cook the Rice

Rinse the basmati rice until the water runs clear. Bring 4 cups water to a boil with salt, bay leaf, cardamoms, cloves, cinnamon, star anise, and 1 tsp oil/ghee. Add rice and cook until 70% done (the grains should still have a bite). Drain immediately to stop cooking.

Step 4: Layer the Biryani

In a heavy-bottomed pot, spread the marinated chicken evenly at the base. Add half the cooked rice on top, then sprinkle mint, coriander, fried onions, saffron milk, and ghee. Repeat with the remaining rice and toppings.

Step 5: Seal and Dum Cook

Cover the pot with foil and a tight-fitting lid to trap steam. Place over medium heat for 5 minutes, then reduce to low and cook for 25-30 minutes (dum method). The steam will gently cook the chicken and infuse the rice with rich flavors.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

Rest the biryani for 10 minutes before opening the lid. Gently fluff the layers with a spatula, keeping the rice grains intact. Serve hot with raita, salad, or mirchi ka salan.

Recipe Notes

Rice quality matters: Use aged basmati rice for that perfect, fluffy texture and long grains.

Marination time: For best flavor, marinate the chicken for at least 1 hour, overnight is even better.

Fried onions tip: Fry the onions on medium heat for even browning. Burnt onions can turn the biryani bitter.

Dum cooking: Keep the heat low during the final steam stage to avoid burning the bottom layer.

Saffron alternative: If saffron isn't available, a pinch of turmeric in warm milk can give a similar golden hue.

Resting time: Let the biryani rest for 10 minutes before serving so the flavors blend beautifully.
